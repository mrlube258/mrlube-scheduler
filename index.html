⚖ Saskatchewan Employment Act — Overtime Rules

Daily Overtime

  • Triggers after >8 hrs in a standard 8-hr day
  • "Day" = calendar day or 24 consecutive hrs from shift start
  • Maximum 16 hrs in any 24-hr period
  • Minimum 8 consecutive hrs rest between shifts

Weekly Overtime

  • Regular week: OT after 40 hours
  • Holiday week: OT after 32 hours
  • Employee receives GREATER of daily or weekly OT
  • OT rate: minimum 1.5× hourly wage

Employee Rights

  • Consent required for >44 hrs/week
  • 24 consecutive hrs off every 7 days (20+ hrs/wk employees)
  • Schedule notice: at least 1 week in advance
  • Reporting-for-duty pay: min 3 hrs even for short shifts

How This Scheduler Complies

  • All shifts exactly 8 hrs → daily OT never triggered
  • FT: max 5 shifts = 40 hrs regular; 4 = 32 hrs holiday week
  • PT: max 2 shifts per week by default (16 hrs)
  • 2+ days off/week → 24 consecutive hrs off guaranteed
  • ZERO scheduled overtime
